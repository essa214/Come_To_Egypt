The largest Sharm El-Sheikh reserves ever, with a total area of ​​650 square kilometers, divided into 500 square kilometers of land and 150 square kilometers of water bodies. Abu Gallum is distinguished from other Sharm El-Sheikh reserves by unstable caves at a depth of 100 meters below sea level with dozens of distinct species of fish, water creatures and coral reefs, which makes it one of the ideal diving sites in Sharm El-Sheikh. Abu Dabab Bay or Dugong

Marsa Alam in the south of Egypt is one of the few places in the world where you can see the dugong, which is a large marine animal of the mammal family and is similar in shape to a manatee, and it is one of the four living creatures of the family of aquatic herbivorous sirens, belonging to the mythology of the nymph The Long Sea and other legends in different cultures. At Abu Dabab, shallow waters and abundant seagrass reserves attract a small number of dugong, also called "sea cows" because of their grass-eating habits. Other residents of the bay that can be seen around the reef include giant sea turtles, parrotfish, angel fish, barracudas and seahorses, while the sandy seabed is home to stingrays.
